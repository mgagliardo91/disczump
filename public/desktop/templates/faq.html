What is disc|zump?
disc|zump is a social disc golf inventory network enabling users to organize, maintain and share collections across any internet-enabled device. Disc golfers can use our site to build and store their collections online for seamless sharing with others, whether it be to show off a recently obtained disc or to provide more detail for a potential trade/sale. Outside of sharing, take advantage of viewing other user's public inventories and don't be scared to shoot them a message when you want to connect.
What is a disc|zump trunk?
A trunk is a user’s inventory of discs. The trunk may be comprised of both private (visible to only the user) and public discs, as well as discs that are on the global marketplace. The trunk terminology derived from the method in which most disc golfers store their discs - in a bag in the back of their car, ready to go at their earliest convenience. Think of a disc|zump trunk as a user “popping their trunk” to show off what they have and what they are ready to sell and trade.
What is the disc|zump marketplace?
Our desire was to not only create a platform that people could use to maintain their digital collection, but also to bring exposure to the individual user no matter where they are located. The disc|zump marketplace does just that. Users can mark discs as For Sale or For Trade and watch as they become available for the world to see.
What are your Terms and Conditions?
Both our Terms and Conditions and our Privacy Policy are publicly available from the footer bar of our website.
Do I need an account?
We have designed disc|zump to be open to the public. Anyone can browse our site without signing up, whether it be to find discs on the marketplace or to check out a user’s trunk. To take advantage of the full features that disc|zump has to offer - creating an inventory, placing discs on the marketplace, messaging users - you will need an account. Luckily, basic accounts are completely free!
Is it free to sign up?
Making a basic account is completely free. We require a valid email address to ensure that you never lose access to your account as well as for system-level notifications (configured in your preferences). Your email address will never be shared publicly. To see more information on the data we collect, take a look at our Privacy Policy.
Are their different tiers of accounts?
disc|zump offers three tiers of accounts, each providing a different level of functionality in connection with our global marketplace. Users will start out as The Freelancer, which gives you full access to build your trunk, send messages to other users and place up to 2 discs on the global marketplace simultaneously. Paid accounts, namely The Entrepreneur and The Chief Executive, provide additional functionality such as an increase in marketplace discs as well as access to marketplace tools (bulk edits, bulk bumps, etc.). See our About page for more information.
How do I upgrade my account?
Account types (upgrades/downgrades) can be modified at your Membership page.
How do I link my account to Facebook?
You can link and unlink your disc|zump account to Facebook at any time by visiting the your account page. Why should you link your account? Linking to Facebook provides you with the ability to log in with a single click and never have to worry about that pesky password field. It also gives you access to adding a Facebook verification (see verifications). We will never post or share on your behalf, so I guess the real answer is… why not?
What browsers are currently supported?
disc|zump implements many newer technologies to create an interface that is user-friendly and feature-rich. Because of this, certain browsers as well as browser versions will work better than others. As of November 1, 2016, we currently support most versions of Chrome, Safari, and Firefox as well as newer versions of Opera and Internet Explorer. Please note that Internet Explorer versions prior to v10 may have limited functionality. We suggest using one of the other major browsers for the best results. Further support will come in the near future, but other areas of development may take priority. See our section on issue reporting for more information.

How do I add a disc to my inventory?
You can add a disc at any point my visiting your trunk page and selecting the Add Disc button in your profile. This will bring you to the Create by Template page, giving you access to search and select the disc you are wanting to add, which will autofill all manufacturer fields for you. If you would like to build the disc from the ground up, you can opt to start from scratch. Look out for icon to get more information about each field.
What is the difference between a public and private disc?
We want to make it easy for you to decide when a disc is visible to the public eye. Mark your disc as public and it can be shared with the world, whether you do so with a link or by sharing directly to Facebook. Mark your disc as private and no one but you can see it. Flexibility is a remarkable thing.

How do I add a disc to the marketplace?
You can add a disc to the marketplace by indicating that the disc is For Sale or For Trade. These tags will automatically place the disc amongst all other marked discs to be viewed, filtered and searched upon in the disc|zump marketplace. Your account type determines how many discs you can place on the marketplace at any given time. A disc must be public and have at least one image to be placed on the marketplace. For more information on account types, see our About page.
What is “bumping” a disc?
When you place a disc on the marketplace, it gets added to the pool of discs that other users are attempting to publicize. The marketplace, by default, will sort marketable discs by when they were added. A recently added disc will maintain its spot within the marketplace for at least a three day period, regardless of modifications, allowing other discs to gain exposure as they are added. After the three day period is up, you may choose to “bump” your disc, resubmitting it to the top of the marketplace.
How do I share a disc?
You can share any public disc by clicking the icon on the disc page. Then you can select one of the two options below:
Copy the shareable link and send it to others.
Share the disc directly to Facebook on your wall, a friends wall, or even directly into a group.
Why should I add tags to my disc collection?
As avid disc golf collectors, we wanted a way to manipulate and navigate our inventories in the most efficient way possible. Use tags to make filtering your collection even easier. Mark a group of discs as "favorites" or "tournament-specific" collectables so that you can quickly view them in the future. Create as many tags as you want, and reuse the same tag to group similar discs together. A single disc can be included in unlimited tag groups. Be creative!

Tag examples include First Run, In The Bag, Halloween Collection, Zone Collection, Tournament Stamp, Glow Bag, and many more...
What are verifications?
disc|zump is a platform for users to connect with each other and potentially set up deals to buy/trade discs. A platform of this nature requires trust in order to work properly. Users can establish trust by proving they are who they claim to be. At this point, we allow two types of verifications, namely Facebook and PDGA. Users will be required to have access to their accounts on each of these sites in order to claim their verification. Adding verifications will expose the public links to these accounts on their trunk, thus allowing other users to conclude if they are a person worth engaging with in a transaction. Check out your current verifications.
Will there be more verifications in the future?
Most definitely. We will work to bring more verification platforms to our users in the future as well as a review/feedback system to allow our users to prove they are worth trusting.
I can't log in, what should I do?
If you are experiencing trouble logging into disc|zump, first try to recover your account by navigating to the Recover page. If you are still having issues, please contact disc|zump Support at support@disczump.com. We will do our best to respond as soon as possible.
Help! I can't access my account!
You can reset your password by accessing the Recover Password page. Enter in the email address associated with your account and we will shoot you an email with instructions. Still unable to access your account? Shoot an email to disc|zump Support and we will see what we can do.
I've found an issue with the site, what should I do?
As a newly released web application, it is possible that you may come across an issue ("bug") that we failed to discover during our testing period. We welcome your input - please shoot us feedback by sending an email to disc|zump Support directly at support@disczump.com. We will do our best to respond as soon as possible.
